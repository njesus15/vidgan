General Adversarial Networks

General Adversarial Networks, also known as GANs, have become very popular in the field of artificial intelligence. First introduced by Ian Goodfellow in 2014, GANs generate realistic synthetic data by learning the latent distribution of the training data. The GANs introduced by Goodfellow are composed of two neural networks: a generator and a discriminator. During training, the goal of the discriminator is to correctly classify it's input as real (actual data) or fake (output of generator), while the generator's goal is to fool the discriminator. Over the past few years there have been many variations of generative adversarial networks in terms of model architecture, training method, and cost functions, but they all aim to achieve one goal: generate realistic synthetic data.

Video GAN Project Overview

Video GAN is an application of GANs that learns how to generate short synthetic videos by learning the dynamics of the training data. The general approach of using generator and discriminator remains the same (see Model), but rather than outputting a single image, the Video GAN generator produces 32 slightly different images. I decided to apply this project on basketball highlight videos, given that it is my favorite sport, and the background generally remains static while there is a lot of movement from the players on the court.
